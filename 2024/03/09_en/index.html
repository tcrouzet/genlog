New Breakthrough Brings Matrix Multiplication Closer to Ideal

William Kuszmaul from Harvard University hails a groundbreaking improvement in matrix multiplication efficiency. The operation, vital in various algorithms for tasks like improving graphics and optimizing network theory, hinges on speed for time and cost savings. Traditional methods entail n3 multiplications for n-by-n matrices. Strassen and Winograd pioneered faster approaches for smaller matrices by decomposing them. New research aims to push the limit and reach the theoretical minimum of n² steps for multiplying matrices.
