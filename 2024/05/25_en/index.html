Harvard researchers create new pill that could reverse the aging process

Scientists from Harvard University have isolated six chemical mixtures with the ability to reverse aging in human and mice skin cells. Dr. David Sinclair, a molecular biologist at Harvard Medical School, views this as a significant step towards affordable whole-body rejuvenation. The groundbreaking research has piqued the interest of influential figures like Elon Musk. The team used a high-throughput screening technique to differentiate younger cells from senescent ones, a marker of aging. They identified specific markers of aging using transcription-based clocks and NCC assays in diverse cell types.
